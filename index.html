Does the crime in Austin go up during SXSW?

Chart.js

This data comes from the Austin Police Incident report. In the data there was TONS of crime reports. However, what I wanted to research was if the crime goes up during SXSW and what is the most common crime. With this chart, I broke the data down by the days of SXSW 2015 which were March 13-22. As you can see the x-axis contains the days and each wave or line represents a different crime with the specific amount for that day of a certain crime. I like this chart, however I don't like that there isn't really any legends and you don't know which crime is which. At the bottom I used highcharts, which I think I like much better.

Google Charts.

Here I used the Google Charts to show what the most common crimes were again, during SXSW. As you can tell by the pie graph, Thefts are the most common, with assaults not too far behind.

Google Fusion Tables.

After seeing the google chart and gathering that theft was the most common crime, I wanted to see if theft was highest in March because of SXSW or was this the lowest in comparison to other months. According to the data the I collected, March was not the highest month for theft, which I thought it would be because of the excess tourism around SXSW. The chart shows that July has the highest amount of thefts followed by August, then March. I'd really like to see what is happening if anything within those months to see if maybe there is some reason why the thefts are so high. Maybe compare it to other years.

Highcharts

Here I almost used the same Data except I just used the most common theft crimes. Each one of these falls into the theft category. The one that is the highest I labeled as "uncategorized" because in the dataset, there was no specific information on what kind of theft was reported. I'd really like to inquire more and find out what exactly these were. I think they would or could possibly change the data more. I really liked this table, I think it is easier to read and looks better visually. It's tricky to get set up though, but I think it adds a more professional look.
